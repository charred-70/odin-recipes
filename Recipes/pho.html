Pho

There are few better comfort foods than Vietnamese phở. When I’m on the verge of a cold or in need of a culinary pick-me-up, I sit down to a restorative bowl of aromatic broth, slippery rice noodles, and fresh, customizable garnishes.

Ingredients

Broth

  • 1 large onion, peeled and halved
  • 2 inch fresh ginger, peeled and halved lengthwise
  • 3 inch cinnamon stick, preferably Vietnamese cassia-cinnamon
  • 1 star anise
  • 2 whole cloves
  • 1 teaspoon coriander seeds
  • 4 cups unsalted vegatable stock or broth
  • 2 teaspoons tamari or soy sauce
  • 4 medium carrots, peeled and corsely chopped

Noodles

  • 8 ounces dried flat rice noodles, width is depended on preference

Toppings

  • Protien, like tofu, or bean curd skin
  • Mushrooms
  • Vegetables, like bok choy or cabbage

Garnishes

  • 1/2 large onion
  • 1 chile pepper
  • 1/2 cup bean sprouts
  • Large handful of herbs, like cilantro or thai basil
  • Hoisin sauce, Siracha

Instructions

  1. Char the onion and ginger over a flame until slightly blackened for 5 minutes on each side
  2. Rinse with water and set aside
  3. Place the cinnamon, star anise, cloves, and cirander in a large pot over medium-low heat and dry-roast, stirring to prevent burning.
  4. Add vegetable stock into the pot
  5. Bring the broth to a boil
  6. Reduce the heat and simmer for 30 minutes
  7. Strain the solids out and simmer until ready to serve, make noodles while the broth simmers
  8. Place noodles in a large bowl and cover it with hot water
  9. Let it stand for 20-30 minutes until tender, but chewy
  10. Drain the bowl
  11. Prepare the toppings as desired - slice and cook tofu, steam vegatables, etc.
  12. Divide the noodles between 2 bowls.
  13. Arrange toppings over noodles.
  14. Ladle the broth between two bowls and serve with garnishes on the side
